My UCF Journey

Intro

My UCF journey is quite a long and turbulent one. Though it started all the way back in 2018, it has taken until now for a clear path forward to emerge for me. For a very long time, I dreaded the moment when I would be leaving college since I had never truly decided what I wanted to do with my life. But this very semester has given me a big wakeup call, and shown me that there really is a path forward for me.

The Past

When I graduated high school, I had only a vague idea of going to university to study tech-related stuff. Unfortunately, my first year at UCF was anything but smooth sailing. Between struggling to keep up with classwork, the lack of a rigid, pre-defined schedule, and a general feeling of isolation, by the time spring semester came around, I could hardly even make myself leave my dorm room to get food, much less to go to class. This was where my first time at UCF would end.

Needless to say, I was very upset at the time. I was legitimately at a loss at what to do with myself. I was left questioning if I was capable of postsecondary education at all. And the most frightening question of all, what would happen to me if I wasn't?

The Present

After several months of recuperation, as well as a diagnosis of neurodivergence and some light soul-searching, I would end up attending Valencia College. During my time there I gradually began to better understand what went wrong before. Through slowly developing strategies to overcome my mental barriers (and finally get a better understanding of how I work) and eventually my academic performance improved to where I was able to graduate with an A.A. degree and get readmitted to UCF.

Since then, I have managed to keep the academic momentum going, and am now within a year of graduating with my bachelor's degree. Though I went at a timid pace at first, my academic pace has gradually quickened as time has gone on.

The Future

To be totally honest, up to this semester I was still quite flexible about what I wanted to do after college. I had at least settled on jobs related to the digital media curriculun here, but I was undecided as to whether to focus on the game dev side or the web dev side. After this semester however, I think the decision has been made clear for me. Through this class (as well as my "Client-Side Scripting") class I've discovered how interesting web development can be, and I do believe I would be quite happy pursuing it as a job after graduation.

I actually find it quite ironic that this is the class and field that has finally stuck for me. ALl the way back in that disatrous first year, the first class I took was an "Intro to C" programming class, which ended up being a disatrous experience for me and pushed me away from programming for years.

Funny how things work out.
